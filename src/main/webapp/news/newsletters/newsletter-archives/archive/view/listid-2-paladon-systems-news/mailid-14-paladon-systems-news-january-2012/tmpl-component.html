Paladon Systems News - January 2012

 
January 2012, Issue 5
 
 
Dear Visitor,
Before talking about this year, I would just like to say a few words about 2011. After a relatively slow start, we finished it in an extremely strong position with orders at a record high. There were many significant commercial achievements, including the project win and start of deliveries for the ConocoPhillips Jasmine Field, and the order completion of the -65°C Lloyds certified electro-hydraulic ball valve actuators units for Gazprom’s Bovanenkovo-Ukhta pipeline in Russia. We also gained numerous new customer approvals, and welcomed two new business partners to the Paladon Systems’ family; Riham in Kuwait and Fluid Control in Norway.
 
In 2012 we look forward to considerable growth, particularly with regards to the formation of HIPPSCO with our technology partners HGSafety and L&N Scotland. HIPPSCO draws together the best-of-breed skills, experience and technology from all three companies to enable the provision of fully documented HIPPS packages. These turnkey packages comprise of integrated valves, actuators, logic solvers and independently certified documentation packages.
 
We also have further geographical expansion planned for 2012, as well as the continued investment in our R&D programs which will result in some exciting new product releases later this year. Of course, all our plans are reliant upon our continued ability to serve our loyal customers with first class products, and in a manner of which we may be proud. Thank you for your time and my very best wishes for the year ahead.

Brian Ennever

Paladon Systems Automates the World’s First 25,000 psig, 450°F (232°C) Control Choke Valve
Paladon Systems has successfully completed an order for the supply of four electro-hydraulic valve positioning systems for choke valves that exceed the operating pressures and temperatures of all other choke valves currently available in the industry. Product Manager, Mike Northwood, “This project was extremely challenging from a technical perspective. Using an existing ring-main hydraulic circuit operating at 4,500 psig and with control via an infrared controller, the linear hydraulic actuators and control systems had to generate 165,000 lbs. of thrust, and position the choke valves with an accuracy of better than 0.5%. Designing the systems to be sufficiently robust to operate reliably in such an extreme application was demanding in itself, but the very low resolution positioning requirements also created significant technical hurdles for our design team. With 30 years of valve positioning experience, this was still a prestigious project win for us; and we were extremely pleased to have been trusted to provide positioning systems for such a critical and severe service application.” Find out more about Paladon Systems’ Positioning Systems.

Equipment Shipped for KOC's Minagish Gas Compressor Station
Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that they have shipped the first batch of actuators and control systems for KOC’s Minagish gas compression station. The total scope of supply includes forty eight spring-return pneumatic actuators for 2” to 24” ball valves with pressure ratings up to 2500; and on/off control systems with partial stroking capability and back-up air receivers to provide three strokes on loss of the main pneumatic supply pressure. Financial Director Laurence Goode, “This is one of the largest orders we have ever received for the Kuwaiti market, and we are proud to have been chosen by Velan ABV to support them with this critical and time-sensitive project. Holding KOC approval since 2004, our ability to meet the extremely short delivery was also instrumental in securing this order, and we are pleased to announce that the first batch has shipped on schedule as expected. We look forward to completing this order next month, and to working closely with our new Kuwaiti agent Riham in 2012 to secure more business in this flourishing market.”
   
Paladon Systems to Automate the Storage of Ten Billion Cubic Feet of Natural Gas
Following a recent site survey of Star Energy’s Humbly Grove facility, Paladon Systems has been chosen to retrofit thirteen 3” to 10” Class 600 to 1500 manual valves with pneumatic spring-return actuators. Each actuator will be fitted with a control system to provide remote open/close valve operation, along with a closed loop breathing system to prevent environmental ingress to the actuator pneumatic cylinders during spring operation. Applications Engineer Malcolm Norton, “We’ve been providing equipment and field services to the Humbly Grove facility since 2005, and we are proud to support this critical part of England’s energy infrastructure throughout the whole retrofit process, from site review through to installation and commissioning.” Located in Hampshire England, oil was originally discovered at Humbly Grove in 1980. Comprising of two oilfields (Great Oolite and Rhaetic), oil production commenced in 1984 and continues to this day. Due to falling reservoir pressures and oil production, in 1995 the first gas injection wells were drilled into the reservoirs. Today, Humbly Grove is primarily used as a gas storage facility. Find out more about Humbly Grove.
 
Paladon Systems Supports South Arne Development Phase 3 Project
Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that it has been selected to supply twenty actuator and control systems for the North and East platforms in the Hess South Arne Development Phase 3 project. Four spring-return hydraulic actuators and control systems are to be fitted to the North platform’s 6” and 10” risers to provide ESD capability on loss of the electrical supply, or in the event of a fire. Manual partial valve stroke testing has also been provided for two of the four hydraulic actuator and controls packages. Sales Manager Bob Curnow, “It was our considerable experience with offshore projects, coupled with the fact that we worked very closely with Dragados Offshore and KBR early in the design phase of the project that were critical in ensuring our selection for this prestigious project. The four hydraulic valve actuators and controls are already almost complete, and the remaining sixteen pneumatic actuator and control system packages for the East platform are on schedule for delivery mid-February.” Operated by Hess since 1994, South Arne is located in the Danish sector (block DK 5604/29) of the North Sea.”
 
Shipments Begin for Chennai - Bangalore Pipeline Project
The first of ten self-contained linear electro-hydraulic actuators has been shipped for IndiaOil’s (IOCL) 290km Chennai - Bangalore pipeline. The systems are to be fitted to 8” Class 900 control valves being supplied by KOSO Valve India and Severn Glocon India. Fully ATEX certified for Class 1, Divisions I & II, Group C & D hazardous areas, the double-acting systems will provide on-demand remote on/off valve control. The units have also been designed to provide two failure modes; fail-close on loss of the control signal, and fail stay-put on loss of power. Contract Engineer – Electro-Hydraulics Francis Harris; “For the last couple of years we have been extremely active in India, with the supply of electro-hydraulic systems for use on oil and gas pipelines and Turbine Bypass Systems. Prior to the construction of this pipeline, all petroleum products had to be shipped via road or rail between Chennai and Bangalore. Both of these methods were time consuming, costly and far from ideal from a safety and environmental perspective; and we are therefore proud to support this important upgrade to India’s energy infrastructure with our environmentally safe technology.” Find out more about electro-hydraulic technology.
