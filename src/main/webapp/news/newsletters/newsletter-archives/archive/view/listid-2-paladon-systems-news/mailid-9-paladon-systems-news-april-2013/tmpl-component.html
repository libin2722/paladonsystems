Beijing Safewell Joins Paladon Systems' Business Partner Network to Support Chinese Market
Formed in 2002, Beijing Safewell Technology has over 35 employees located in their Beijing headquarters and Shanghai, Shenzhen, Xian and Hong Kong offices. Beijing Safewell Technology’s core business is providing valves and control systems for China’s oil and gas industry; however, they also have significant experience providing safety systems and instrumentation to other industries including pipeline, refining and chemical. Beijing Safewell Technology Commercial Manager Jenny Hu, “We have represented various valve companies in China for many years including Orion and Viar Valvole; however, we realised that we would need to also partner with a respected valve actuator manufacturer in order to better support our clients in the future. Paladon Systems has the comprehensive actuator product ranges our customers require as well as unique technologies that we believe will prove of significant interest to the major Chinese energy companies.”    

 
Paladon Systems to Supply Electro-Hydraulic Systems for South Stream Pipeline Project
Paladon Systems has been awarded an order from PetrolValves for the supply of 20 ultralow temperature self-contained electro-hydraulic valve actuator systems for Gazprom’s South Stream pipeline project. Fitted to 8” and 16” Class 2500 ball valves, the systems will provide remote on/off valve control and be Lloyds certified for operation down to -65°C. Product Manager Mike Northwood, “This is the latest sizable order for Gazprom following the successful design and operation of the electro-hydraulic units we supplied a couple of years ago for Gazprom’s Bovanenkovo-Ukhta pipeline project. Our electro-hydraulic units are already Gazprom, Lloyds and GOST certified and have operated without any issues for the last two years in one of the harshest environments on the planet. We are pleased to note Gazprom’s continued confidence in our design and supply capabilities and look forward to working closely with PetrolValves and Gazprom to meet the challenging 20 week delivery requirement.” Find out more about Paladon Systems electro-hydraulic units.
 
 

Paladon Systems Approved to Supply Actuators for the Wafra Oilfield
Located on the Kuwait and Saudi Arabia border, the Wafra oilfield first started producing heavy crude in the 1950s. After decades of production the oilfield started to become unprofitable as reserves fell and subsequently the cost of production began to rise. In a joint operation between Chevron and the Kuwait Gulf Oil Company (KGOC), since 2009 a pilot project comprising of 16 steam injection wells has been underway to determine whether steam injection recovery could be applied profitably to the entire field. The results from the pilot project are looking extremely promising with some estimates saying that an additional 1,000 billion barrels (approximately 30% of the entire Middle East oil reserves) could be recovered if steam injection was adopted across the entire field. Find out more about steam injection. 
 

Ammonia Transportation Project Win
Paladon Systems to supply low temperature self-contained electro-hydraulic positioning systems to support the distribution of ammonia in Ukraine. To be fitted to 2” to 6” Class 600 to 1500 globe valves, the self-contained electro-hydraulic systems will provide positioning control of the valves with an accuracy of ±0.5%. The systems will incorporate spring-return linear actuators to provide a valve fail position on loss of the 4-20mA command signal or power supply and be fitted with manual overrides. To be manufactured from 316SS, the ATEX EExd certified systems are required to operate in severe operating conditions with a -45°C to +60°C temperature range. Find out more about electro-hydraulic and positioning systems.
