    July 2016, Issue 23

Dear Visitor,

In the last newsletter I referred to the cold wind blowing through our Industry, well, from our perspective the wind has reduced to a breeze and the temperature has warmed up a little. We enjoyed a successful exhibition in Tehran at their annual Oil and Gas Show in May with business prospects looking quite bright with our local agent, Eshteal Energy Engineering.
 
We have completed the upgrade of the offices in the Staveley Way facility which now includes a good sized conference room with all the latest technological innovations for conferencing and training. This will be the launch pad for our Virtual Reality training workshop, see below for more details.
 
As I hinted earlier we are seeing an upturn in activity but not across the World; some areas are starting to move forward with projects while others remain stubbornly quiet. We are seeing growing interest and, in many areas, acceptance of our new and innovative technologies which, I have to say, is a massive relief having spent not inconsiderable resources on product and system research and development during the last 5 years.
 
Thank you for your continued support.
 
Brian Ennever
Petroleum Development Oman’s Yibal Khuff Field Project Win
To be fitted to 4” class 2500 choke valves, Paladon Systems has been awarded the order to supply 28 ring-main electro-hydraulic positioning systems. The systems will provide precise positioning of the choke valves and a fail close position on loss of the command signal, electrical or hydraulic supplies. ATEX EExd certified, the systems will be controlled using Foundation Fieldbus communications. Find out more about valve positioning systems.
1000 Companies to Inspire Britain Award
Paladon Systems is proud to announce that they have been listed in the, "1000 Companies to Inspire Britain 2016" report. To be listed in this prestigious report, companies had to demonstrate not only positive growth in revenue over the last four years, but also that they significantly outperformed their sector peers. The result is a list of companies richer and more varied than ever identified in any other exercise of this type. Read the report.
World’s First Valve Automation Virtual Reality Training
Designed for the HTC Vive virtual reality headset, this fully interactive training material allows users to assemble a ball valve and double-acting scotch-yoke actuator. Once assembled, users can operate the actuator to see exactly how a scotch-yoke mechanism opens and closes ball valves. Find out more about how you can experience this cutting-edge training medium.
Hydrocarbon Engineering Covers Paladon Systems Turnkey HIPPS Solutions
Find out why High Integrity Pressure Protection Systems (HIPPS) are becoming the over-pressure protection solution of choice and how the lead times and procurement costs of these critical safety systems can be reduced when partnering with a turnkey supplier. Read the article.
ENI Approves Paladon Systems’ Subsea HIPPS Packages
Following on from ENI’s recent decision to start using Paladon Systems electro-hydraulic systems on their offshore platforms, Paladon Systems’ High Integrity Pressure Protection Systems are now approved by ENI for subsea applications. Watch a video tutorial on HIPPS.
Al Dabb’iya Surface Facilities Phase III Project Win
To be fitted to control valves, Paladon Systems will be supplying 20 ring-main powered electro-hydraulic fail-close valve positioning systems. Located 50km south west from Abu Dhabi and located in a shallow marine area, ADCO's Al Dabb’iya field is one of the three fields that comprise the North East Bab (NEB) Development. Phase III of the Al Dabb’iya field development is expected to deliver an additional 73 MBOPD by 2017.

Enhanced Project Management Capabilities
Paladon Systems’ is proud to announce that Contract Manager, Mary Shepherd and Contracts Engineer, Michael Aveling have how obtained their PRINCE2 (Projects IN Controlled Environments) Foundation certificates. PRINCE2 is a process-based method for effective project management and the de facto standard used extensively by the UK Government. PRINCE2 is also widely recognised and used in the private sector, both in the UK and abroad.
