Dear Visitor,

Winter is officially over but a cold wind continues to blast through our industry. For everybody business is tough and especially so in the Northern Hemisphere; so our attention is drawn to sunnier climes.
 
Next month we are going to the Iran Oil and Gas Exhibition in Tehran where we will be exhibiting with our agent, Eshteal in Hall 44B on Stand 1305 from the 5th to the 8th of May. We have important meetings arranged with numerous operating companies during the exhibition.
 
The sunny theme continues with important contract wins in Kuwait, Abu Dhabi and Italy; see below for details.
 
As the price of oil continues to climb slowly there are signs of increasing activity in the market, but we do not expect to see any significant upturn in 2016. In preparation for the expected increase in business we are currently refurbishing and expanding our facilities in Brixworth, UK. 
 
Our product development program continues at some considerable pace and I expect to be announcing some significant changes to our manufacturing capability in the next newsletter. In the meantime I thank you for your continued support.
 
Brian Ennever
Expansion & Revamping of Ahmadi Depot Project Win
Paladon Systems is proud to announce that they have received their first ever order from Kuwait National Petroleum Company (KNPC). To be fitted to 6” to 12” Class 300 gate valves, Paladon Systems will be supplying 18 self-contained electro-hydraulic systems with intumescent fire protection to provide on/off valve control. In addition to remote control, the systems will be fitted with local push button stations and hydraulic hand pumps for local operation.
Meet Us Next Week at the Iran Oil Show
Paladon Systems will be exhibiting (Hall 44B, Stand 1305) at the 21st Iran International Oil, Gas and Petrochemical Exhibition from 5th to 8th May. On display will be our latest Gas-Over-Oil system design which incorporates numerous changes across the entire system. In addition to improved system reliability and robustness, these changes have resulted in reduced manufacturing costs which we are now passing on to our clients.
ENI Installs First Electro-Hydraulic System on Offshore Platform
Historically ENI has typically used pneumatic systems on their offshore platforms; however, Paladon Systems is proud to announce that ENI has now successfully installed and operated an electro-hydraulic system on one of their platforms. Fitted to a 6” Class 900 control valve, the system provides remote valve positioning control. Paladon Italia Sales Engineer Silvia Bettinardi, “We have worked closely with GE Energy throughout this project and are delighted that they recommended our electro-hydraulic technology to ENI; despite the fact that ENI has traditionally relied on pneumatic automation systems. With the system now installed and operating flawlessly, we look forward to working with GE Energy and ENI to deploy our technology throughout all of ENI’s offshore operations.” Find out more about electro-hydraulic positioning systems.
Building Enhancements Start at Paladon Systems’ UK facilities
Scheduled to take nine weeks, building work has started this month to renovate and join Paladon Systems’ two adjacent facilities in Brixworth UK. The renovation work will include the creation of a new reception area, a larger boardroom and several offices. New toilet and eating facilities will be installed as well as a dedicated room to house exhibition units. A covered walkway will connect the two facilities.
High Integrity Pressure Protection Systems Video Tutorial
As Operators are forced to operate in environments of ever increasing severity and risk, the use of High Integrity Pressure Protection Systems (HIPPS) has been rapidly increasing over the last few years. Paladon Systems is able to provide turn-key SIL 3 certified HIPPS and has created a video to help explain where these ultra-reliable systems are used and how they operate. Watch the video.
Satah Al Razboot Field Development Project Win
To be fitted to 4” to 8” ANSI 600 to API 10,000 ball valves, Paladon Italia will be supplying a combination of double-acting and spring-return actuator systems. All systems are required to provide extremely fast valve opening times and are therefore fitted with quick exhaust valves and adjustable air dampers to allow for the valve stroking speeds to be slowed during the final degrees of valve opening. The double-acting systems are also fitted with volume tanks to provide sufficient pneumatic flow rates to ensure rapid valve operation and a valve fail position on loss of the ring main pneumatic supply.
Sponsoring the Next Generation of Instrumentation Engineers
Held earlier this month at the Lancaster Hotel in London, Paladon Systems was proud to host a table at the Institute of Measurement and Control’s (InstMC) 49th annual dinner. Founded in 1944 and incorporated by Royal Charter in 1975, The Institute of Measurement and Control is committed to promoting the professional excellence and standing of engineers and technologists at all levels in the automation, instrumentation, control and related industries.
