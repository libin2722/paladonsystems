Dear Visitor,
 
 
Closing out our financial year at the end of September, I am pleased to advise that we had another successful year with some notable highlights. The successful completion of the large ConocoPhillips Jasmine Development contract and the supply of stainless steel scotch-yoke actuators for the West Azeri platform were two standout successes for me.
 
Our on-going investment in R&D also continues to enable us to offer our customers the most advanced and cost effective solutions, with the latest being the release of the new modular gas-over-oil actuator range in April. Speaking of advanced solutions, this month we received an order for an ASV with an automatic linebreak capability (see below). As well as being our first ASV order in Asia Pacific, what makes this order so noteworthy is that the ASV will be powered by the gas pressure in a subsea pipeline which, to the best of our knowledge, will be the first subsea actuator in the World to be powered this way.
 
We continue to invest heavily in our people and our infrastructure; most recently with several new hires (see below) and updated hardware and CAD software for our engineering teams which will enable us to submit and revise order documentation much faster than was previously possible.
 
Looking forward to the year ahead, we have started strongly with some significant new business and before Christmas we’ll be making some major announcements regarding our manufacturing facilities and market initiatives in the US. In the interim, I urge you to check our website regularly as it is updated weekly with the latest Company and project developments.
 
Brian Ennever
 
 
 
Paladon Systems to Supply ASV with Automatic Linebreak Capability for the Malay Basin Project
To be mounted on a 10” Class 1500 subsea ball valve located at 50 metres water depth, the ASV will monitor pipeline gas pressure and close the valve in the event of a linebreak condition. The 10” subsea line will be used to connect Exxon Mobil’s Lawit platform to an FPSO. Product Manager Mike Northwood, “In addition to the linebreak function, what makes this application interesting is that the ASV will be powered by the gas pressure in the subsea pipeline itself. The ASV was originally designed to be powered using the pressure in subsea oil pipelines; however, the flexibility of the design allows us to power it using gas with minor modification. Exxon Mobil chose our solution instead of the traditional method which relies on buoy mounted hydraulic power units (HPUs) to provide the actuator’s power. Due to the harsh marine operating environment and the general system complexity, buoy mounted HPUs are well known to have issues with operation reliability, and the need to connect them to subsea hydraulic actuators using fragile flexible umbilicals further reduces system availability. Our system is completely self-contained and, being fully subsea, means that it is not exposed to any harsh surface conditions; both of which help ensure that the ASV provides the most reliable solution available to end users today for these types of applications. Of course, it is not just the increased uptime that operators appreciate, it is also the step change in environmental protection that the ASV offers when compared to the traditional solutions.” Find out more about the ASV.

Paladon Systems to Supply 78 Actuators and Controls Systems for the Solan Platform
To be operated and developed by Premier Oil and Chrysaor and located west of Shetland in 137 metres water depth, the not permanently manned installation (NPMI) is expected to provide a peak flowrate of 28,000 bps of oil. Paladon Systems has been tasked with supplying spring-return pneumatic scotch-yoke actuators and control systems for the platform’s 2” to 10” and ANSI 150 to 2500 shutdown, blowdown and ESD ball valves. A further award for the actuation of the platform's butterfly valves is also imminent. The various control systems will provide remote electric open and close control, partial valve stroke testing capability and include air receivers to provide 3 valve strokes in the event of loss of the main pneumatic supply source. Sales Manager Bob Curnow, “We are delighted to have been selected by the platform’s topside engineering contractor ODE to provide the actuators and controls for this platform; especially when considering that we were involved in this project when it was just a conceptual design being carried out by SLP. We have worked with ODE on numerous projects over the past years and it was their previous positive experiences of working with us and the fact that we met 100% of the demanding specification that resulted in us being awarded this large contract. We look forward to continuing our close relationship with ODE and to the successful completion of this project.” Find out more about scotch-yoke actuators and partial valve stroke testing.

Paladon Systems to Support Burun Oil & Gas Field Development in Turmenistan
Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that they have been awarded an order for over 30 actuators and control systems for the Burun Field Oil Development project. The Burun field is located in the Nebit Dag area of Western Turkmenistan and comprises of approximately 150 oil and gas wells with a current total output of 25,000 BOPD. The scope of supply includes pneumatic spring-return and double-acting scotch-yoke actuators and control systems for operation of 2” to 12” ESD and BDV ball valves. All systems will be supplied with mechanical partial stroke testing capability and the double-acting actuators fitted with volume tanks to provide 3 valve strokes in the event of loss of the main pneumatic supply source. Sales Manager Bob Curnow, “We have been bidding this project for about a year and are therefore extremely proud to have now been awarded the order from LFF Scotland. This is the first very large order that we have received from LFF and we look forward to working closely with Valvenco (LFF’s valve automation arm) to ensure the successful completion of this prestigious project.” Find out more about scotch-yoke actuators.
