Redesigned Gas-Over-Oil Systems Now in Production
Following eighteen months of development, Paladon Systems is proud to announce that the first of their redesigned gas-over-oil (also commonly referred to as, "gas/hydraulic") actuator systems have just passed rigorous customer testing and been shipped. Supplied for a compressor station in AGP’s Kazakhstan-China gas pipeline, the two systems were fitted to 4” ANSI 600 full bore Za.ve.ro ball valves. The control systems provide local and remote open and close valve control, as well as a remote ESD capability. Inside Sales Engineer Silvia Bettinardi, “Although we are seeing many parts of the world changing from traditional gas-over-oil to electro-hydraulic actuator systems for gas transmission pipeline applications, there is still a huge market for the traditional technology. Our newly designed gas-over-oil actuator systems are fully manifolded, giving our customers a much more reliable and compact solution than those currently available from our competitors. In addition, the fully manifolded and modular design allows the systems to be very commercially competitive when compared to designs that employ significant amounts of external piping to connect the various control components. The modular design also allows our customers to make quick and inexpensive changes in the field to the control system logic by simply adding or changing the various control modules.”
 
Paladon System's Protects Power Generation Turbines in Three Countries
Paladon Systems is pleased to announce the completion of their latest order for Turbine Bypass Systems. Supplied to SPX, the order consisted of three integrated Siemens PLC Control Panels and Hydraulic Power Units, and eight fast operating Linear Actuators fitted with Positional Control Systems. The equipment will be installed in conventional power stations located in Korea, Vietnam and Singapore; and will be used to protect the steam turbines during their critical start-up and shut-down operating phases. Product Manager Mike Northwood, “We are thrilled to see that the many benefits of Paladon Systems’ leak-free hydraulic positioning technology are now being recognised globally by conventional power station operators. When compared to the hydraulic positioning systems previously used in power stations, our technology offers operators significant OPEX savings, as well as providing enhanced system reliability and plant safety. As the Product Manager for Turbine Bypass Systems, it is extremely satisfying for me to see the results of our R&D efforts being successfully deployed around the world; and of being part of this rapidly growing part of our business.” Find out more about Turbine Bypass Systems.

Paladon Systems Upgrades a 12 Year Old Electro-Hydraulic System to a HIPPS 
Applications Engineer Malcolm Norton, “Back in 2000 we installed an electro-hydraulic actuator system on a 36” gas pipeline that connects National Grid’s Carnforth and Nether Kellet compressor stations. The main function of the system was to protect the Carnforth site from over-pressurization by monitoring the pipeline’s pressure, and closing the mainline ball valve in the event that 2 out of 3 pressure pilot valves detected a high pressure event. The application was extremely technically challenging as we were asked to design a system that relied purely on hydraulic pressure pilot and pilot valves to provide 2oo3 logic solving capability. After 12 years of operation, National Grid wanted to enhance the reliability of the system and add data logging and data analysis capabilities by replacing the hydraulic logic system with electronic pressure sensors connected to a logic solver located in a safe area. We are pleased to maintain our long track-record of providing excellent support for National Grid by successfully retrofitting the HIPPS on schedule.” Find out more about electro-hydraulic systems and HIPPS packages.
