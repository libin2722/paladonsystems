Dear Visitor,

Oh my goodness me! Apparently it’s all doom and gloom in the Oil and Gas industry. Forecasts from various learned bodies would have us cutting our throats at the short term prospects, but all is not so. Certainly there are areas where the downturn will affect our business, but equally there are still many opportunities for us to grow.
 
One key area of our business model which contributes to our continued success even during industry downturns is product development, particularly with regards to ever cleaner and more energy efficient systems. Released to market at the end of last year, our latest advancement is the development of a range of compact electro-hydraulic systems (see below) which are typically 30% smaller and 25% lighter than competing designs, as well as being far more energy efficient and costing less.
 
With regard to expanding our customer sales and service support, we will be opening our newest office in Abu Dhabi in February and, since the last newsletter, we have appointed a new agent in South Korea, Yooshin E&I (see below).
 
Thank you for your support in 2014 and I will be contacting you again in April with news regarding some positive company restructuring.
 
Brian Ennever
 
Ultra-Compact Systems Unveiled at Valve World 2014
During Valve World 2014 held in December last year, Paladon Systems unveiled their latest electro-hydraulic product range; ultra-compact systems that utilise the latest in motor/pump, positioner/re-transmitter and communication technologies. Being 30% smaller and 25% lighter than competing electro-hydraulic systems, these systems gained considerable interest from major end users as well as the press. Watch a video interview with Product Manager Mike Northwood and download the product datasheet.
Kraken Field FPSO Major Project Win
To be fitted to 2” to 24” Class 150 to 2500 ball valves, Paladon Systems will be supplying 91 pneumatic double-acting and spring-return scotch-yoke valve actuators and control systems. The fully stainless steel control systems will provide remote electric open/close valve control and smart valve positioning via 4-20m positioners using HART communications. Operated and owned by EnQuest (60%), Cairn Energy (25%) and First Oil (15%), the Kraken field is located approximately 400km north east of Aberdeen in 120m water depth and is anticipated to hold 140 million barrels of heavy oil with a production life of up to 25 years. Currently in construction in Singapore, the Kraken FPSO will be one of the largest vessels ever built for the North Sea. Using a converted Surzmax ice-class tanker, the FPSO will be able to store 600,000 barrels of heavy oil being supplied from 16 risers. Find out more about FPSO vessels.
