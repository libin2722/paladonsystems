Paladon Systems News - April 2014

 

 
April 2014, Issue 14  
 
 

Dear Visitor,

Our year continues at a rapid pace with the first deliveries out of our Malaysian facility and the opening of a new office in Aberdeen.
 
As promised in the last Newsletter, I can now draw your attention to the latest enhancements we’ve made to our Self-Contained Electro-Hydraulic systems. Traditionally these systems have been reasonably complex in terms of the mounting and tubing of the many components; however, our latest designs integrate the motor, pump and controls into a single assembly; resulting in considerable cost savings and higher reliability due to the dramatic reduction in potential leak paths. We have also integrated the latest motor technologies to reduce power requirements down to as little as 100 Watts and included Bluetooth communication for system configuration and diagnostics; as well as providing support for common communication protocols including HART and Foundation Fieldbus.
 
These systems are truly environmentally friendly with low power requirements, zero emissions and the ability to operate on biodegradable fluids whilst still maintaining the advantages of fail-safe or fail-last functions for on/off or positional valve applications.
 
Next week we’ll be exhibiting one of these systems at OTC, we hope to meet you there to give you a demonstration and answer any questions you may have.
 
Thank you for your continued support, I will be in touch with you again in July’s newsletter.
 
Brian Ennever

North Sea HIPPS Project Win
Paladon Systems has secured the contract to provide HIPPS actuators and control system packages for TAQA's Cladhan Topside Modifications project. To be fitted to 2” to 10” Class 1500 ball valves, Paladon Systems will be responsible for the supply of four pneumatic quarter-turn actuators and control systems. To support SIL certification, the controls will include dual solenoid valves for redundancy and a partial valve stroke testing function via HART communication. 4-20mA position feedback will also be integrated into each control package. Find out more about HIPPS and partial valve stroke testing.

First Order Completed by Paladon Systems' Malaysian Facility
Fitted to 3” and 4” Class 2500 control valves and comprising of 30 Self-Contained Electro-Hydraulic positioning systems, Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that their Malaysian facility has just completed its first order. Responsible for valve integration and final assembly configuration and testing for this particular order, the Malaysian facility will continue to ramp up its staff and capabilities throughout 2014 to provide comprehensive sales, assembly and after-sales support services. Vice President – Sales Asia Pacific Region Anba Nagappan, “This order is a milestone in regards to Paladon Systems being able to provide prompt support for its increasing customer base in the Far East. With the ability to integrate valves and test the complete assemblies close to where they will be installed, we can now offer valve companies reduced lead times and freight costs for projects located in the Far East.” Contact Paladon Systems’ Malaysian facility to discuss your local valve automation requirements.
 
Paladon Systems successfully completes its largest order in its 33 year history
Paladon Systems is proud to announce that it has shipped the last batch of hydraulic power units and control systems for Shell's Bacton Terminal Rejuvenation project. Comprising of seven hydraulic power units, eighteen accumulator banks and local control systems, Paladon Systems was also contracted to assist with various onsite activities including hydraulic flushing, installation and commissioning. Opened in 1968, the Bacton Terminal is located in North Norfolk, UK and takes gas from the Southern North Sea (SNS) and distributes it to the UK, Belgium and the Netherlands. Find out more about Hydraulic Power Units.
 
Electro-Hydraulic Systems Support Increased for the US
Paladon Americas is pleased to announce the hire of Gary Robbins in the position of Applications Engineer. Working closely with Paladon Systems product development team in the UK, Gary will be primarily responsible for designing electro-hydraulic systems for the US market’s specific requirements. Starting in 1981 as an Applications Engineer for GH Bettis, Gary has remained in the valve automation industry ever since. He has worked for several actuator manufacturers and distributors as well as co-founding Automation Technology Inc. (ATI) in 1995. Gary has extensive valve automation experience with all types of linear and quarter-turn valve applications, including advanced satellite controlled and solar powered electro-hydraulic systems. Find out more about Paladon Americas and electro-hydraulic systems.
