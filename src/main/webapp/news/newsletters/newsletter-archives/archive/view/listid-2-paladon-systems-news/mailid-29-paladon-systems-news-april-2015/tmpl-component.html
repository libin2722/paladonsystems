   April 2015, Issue 18

Dear Visitor,

Life continues at a significant pace. My congratulations to both Brian Simister and Mike Northwood on their promotions and a very warm welcome to Lynette Ross and Mustakim Ali who have recently joined our team.
 
During the coming weeks there is much travel on my agenda; starting with the Iranian Oil & Gas Show in Tehran, followed by the new Industrial Valve Summit in Bergamo and then on to Kuala Lumpur for the Asian Oil, Gas and Petrochemical Engineering Exhibition in early June. At all of these shows we will be exhibiting the latest valve automation technology, with a special emphasis on our new compact linear and quarter-turn electro-hydraulic systems.
 
Back home I can report the successful completion of our planning application to build a new reception area at Brixworth which will also serve as a link building between our two factories.
 
While we have seen a certain downturn in business it seems to be restricted to certain geographical areas, whereas other parts of the World continue to be very active. Being a relatively small company, we are very flexible and have redirected our efforts into the more productive areas.
 
I thank you all for your continued support which is enabling us to further invest in technology and resources. I look forward to being in contact with you again in our July newsletter.
 
Brian Ennever
Meet Us at IVS & OGA
Paladon Systems will be exhibiting at IVS (stand 49) on 27th and 28th May in Bergamo, Italy and at OGA (stand 5705) from 2nd to 4th June in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. On display will be self-contained electro-hydraulic linear positioning systems, solar powered self-contained electro-hydraulic quarter-turn positioning systems as well as Paladon Systems’ latest manual override design. Throughout the exhibitions you will be able to see these systems in action and we welcome you to meet with us to find out how they can benefit your operations.
ADOC Hail Offshore Oilfield Project Win
To be fitted to six CC20 Cameron control choke valves, the compact self-contained electro-hydraulic systems will provide valve positioning with an accuracy of ±0.5% using 4-20mA HART communications and a fail-closed valve position on loss of either the electric power supply or command signal. The Hail offshore oilfield is located approximately 10km south of Mubarraz Island and is expected to contain sufficient reserves to last for the next 30 years. Download the compact self-contained electro-hydraulic positioning system datasheet.

Petronas Product Approval
Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that their entire range of actuators and control systems have now been approved by Petronas. Vice President – Sales Asia Pacific Region, Anba Nagappan, “Paladon Systems has been active in Malaysia for many years; however, we have been rapidly increasing our local market share since setting up our local service facility and partnering with Songket Petroleum last year. Since Petronas is responsible for all of Malaysia’s oil and gas resources, getting their product approval was an obvious and critical step towards developing the Malaysian market further. Songket Petroleum’s assistance throughout Petronas’ stringent product approval process was invaluable and so I’d like to take this opportunity to thank their entire team for their efforts." Find out more about Petronas and Songket Petroleum.
Enhanced Customer Support from Project Pre-Feed through to Delivery
Paladon Systems is delighted to announce the latest addition to their external sales team, Lynette Ross in the role of Sales Manager Projects. Reporting to Managing Director, Brian Ennever, Lynette will be responsible for identifying potential projects as early as their pre-feed stage and will then work closely with the project design teams to help them identify process improvements through the implementation of industry-leading valve automation technologies. Lynette has 20 years of experience in the valve and valve automation industry and has spent the last six years based in Australia supporting the Australasian and South East Asian markets.
Paladon Systems Starts Technology Roadshows in England
In February Paladon Systems started conducting technology roadshows in England to demonstrate their compact self-contained electro-hydraulic systems. Sales Manager, Stuart Bailey, "When we unveiled this technology at Valve World in December last year, it generated so much interest that we thought that customers who couldn't attend Valve World would also appreciate seeing the system in action. Knowing that it can be difficult for our customers to find the time to visit our UK Headquarters, we decided to take the technology to them." Contact Stuart Bailey to arrange a demonstration.
New Sales Manager Hired for Clients Based in the Middle East
Reporting to Vice President - Sales Asia Pacific Region Anba Nagappan, Paladon Systems is pleased to announce the hire of Mustakim Ali in the position of Regional Sales Manager (Middle East). Anba Nagappan, “With Paladon Systems now holding approvals from nearly all of the major energy company headquartered in the Middle East, we needed to increase our local support for them, as well as for our agents who are spread throughout the region. Previously working for our UAE agent, Falcor Engineering & Contract Services, Ali has over eight years’ experience with valves and valve automation systems and this will prove invaluable with supporting our sales growth in this very active region.”
New Manager for Electro-Hydraulic Systems
Mike Northwood has been promoted from Product Manager to Business & Product Development Manager for Paladon Systems' electro-hydraulic product lines. Mike Northwood, "Although I will continue to be involved with the continued research and development of electro-hydraulic systems, with the significant increase in Paladon Systems resources dedicated to this technology during the last 12 years I will now be able to focus on introducing these systems to new customers and markets. Malaysia and India have always been strong markets for Paladon Systems’ standard pneumatic and hydraulic systems; however, with Petronas and CCOE recently approving our electro-hydraulic systems, with the right focus I foresee that the sales of Paladon Systems’ electro-hydraulic systems will surpass those of our standard systems within the next 2 to 3 years."
