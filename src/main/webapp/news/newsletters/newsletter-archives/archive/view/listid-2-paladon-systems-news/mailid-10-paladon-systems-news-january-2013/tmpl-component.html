Paladon Systems News - January 2013

 
January 2013, Issue 9
 
Dear Visitor,
 
I can now announce the outcome of an initiative that has been underway for nearly a year now. Although having some success in the US market, we knew that to gain a significant share of this market we would have to overcome the shipping disadvantages of being a European manufacturer. The preferred approach was to partner with an established US company who could stock actuators and who had the skills and experience necessary to assemble complete valve, actuator and control system packages; we are pleased to announce that we have found this partner.
 
Rekindling business relationships that go back more than 20 years, Bakersfield Pipe and Supply (BPS) and Paladon Systems have come together to operate in the US under the name of Paladon Americas. Paladon Americas has already established an engineering and assembly facility in Houston and, with an actuator stock in excess of one million dollars, begun taking orders from customers who appreciate the short lead times and coast-to-coast support.
 
I am also pleased to see the return of two senior managers to Paladon Systems. Brian Simister who will head up all UK based engineering activities and Stuart Bailey who will support the sales of pipeline equipment into the UK market.
 
Another positive development is the recent acquisition of an additional UK facility. The facility will strengthen our ability to manage large and complex projects and is a critical step in ensuring the continued expansion of Paladon Systems' capabilities.
 
In closing, these are exciting times and we sincerely appreciate all your support that has made this progress possible.
 
Brian Ennever
 
 
Valve Actuator Stock in Excess of 1 Million Dollars Drastically Reduces Lead Times in the US
Bakersfield Pipe & Supply (BPS) announce that they have partnered with UK valve actuator manufacturer and control systems specialist Paladon Systems to supply all quarter-turn and linear valve actuators for the US market. Bakersfield Pipe & Supply President Dan Byrum, “One of BPS’ key strengths is making sure that we stock the products that our customers typically require. After stocking pipe, valves and fittings for many years, we realized that stocking a comprehensive range of valve actuators would complement these product lines and increase the total value we bring to our customers’ operations. In the last five years we have also noticed a decline in domestic valve actuator manufacturing and this has resulted in increased lead times for customers. By investing in a large stock of valve actuators, we can provide significantly reduced lead times when compared to other valve actuator suppliers. Paladon Systems' actuators are also fully modular, so that allows us to stock numerous sizes of cylinders, spring-cartridges and scotch-yoke housings to meet the vast majority of valve torque characteristics we typically see, and offer optimal actuator sizes to reduce the cost and size to our customers.” Read the complete press release.
 

Enhanced Plant Safety and Environmental Performance at Shell’s Bacton Gas Terminal
After conducting several site surveys, Paladon Systems has been chosen by Jacobs Engineering on behalf of Shell UK to assist with upgrading the safety of Bacton’s shutdown system to SIL 2. Paladon Systems will be supplying 8 Hydraulic Power Units (HPUs) to provide hydraulic power for 24 linear and rotary double-acting and spring-return valve actuators fitted to 3” to 30” Class 600 and 900 shutdown valves. Each HPU will be required to supply sufficient hydraulic flow rates and pressures to power up to 7 valve actuators simultaneously and will be replacing existing gas-over-oil systems. Opened in 1968, the Bacton Terminal is located in North Norfolk, UK and takes gas from the Southern North Sea (SNS) and distributes it to the UK, Belgium and the Netherlands. Find out more about HPUs.
 
Additional Assembly and Testing Facility Acquired
Paladon Systems has recently purchased a new 5,500 square feet assembly and testing facility located directly next to their existing headquarters in Brixworth, England. Managing Director Brian Ennever, “The requirement for additional assembly and testing areas started to become necessary in 2012 due to not only an increase in general product sales, but also due to an increase in the physical size of the systems that we have started supplying, specifically hydraulic power units and HIPPS packages. This additional facility will make the management of large volume and larger sized system orders much more efficient, ultimately resulting in reduced lead times for our customers. We are also in the process of acquiring a new manufacturing plant in Italy and more information on this will follow shortly.”
 

Panipat-Rewari Product Pipeline Augmentation Project Win
Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that they have been awarded their latest order with Severn Glocon India for the supply of linear positioning systems. Being fitted to 4” and 6” Class 600 pressure control valves, the explosion-proof systems will provide accurate valve positioning, high and low system pressure alarms and local manual override control. Product Manager Mike Northwood, “One of the real benefits of self-contained electro-hydraulic systems is that they have the low installation costs typically associated with electric actuators, but provide the high torque or thrust outputs historically only available from purely pneumatic or hydraulic systems. Self-contained electro-hydraulic systems can also provide a failure position on loss of the supply pressure via either a hydraulic accumulator or a spring-return actuator, unlike electric actuators which fail last (stay put) on loss of their electrical supply.” Operated by the Indian Oil Corporation (IOCL), the 155km long 12“ Panipat-Rewari petroleum product pipeline (PRPL) was originally commissioned in September 2004 for the transportation of petroleum products from Panipat Refinery to the agricultural and industrial demand areas of Haryana. Find out more about self-contained electro-hydraulic systems.
 

Shah Gas Development Project Win
Paladon Systems has been awarded an order to provide 22 electro-hydraulic linear positioning systems for ADNOC’s Shah Gas Development project. The double-acting linear actuators and control systems will be fitted to 8” Class 2500 production choke valves to provide precise valve positioning control via HART communication with an accuracy of ±0.5%. The stainless steel systems will be powered using a ring-main hydraulic supply with pressures up to 207 Barg and be designed to operate in ambient operating temperatures up to +58°C. Designed to fail stay-put (fail-last) on loss of the hydraulic supply, electrical supply or command signal, the systems will be fitted with hydraulic handpumps to allow for local manual control. Product Manager Mike Northwood, “Having recently demonstrated our positioning technology at the ADIPEC exhibition in Abu Dhabi, we are pleased to see that our hydraulic valve positioning systems are increasingly being considered by the major end users in the Middle East as the preferred solution.” The goal of the Shah Gas Development project is to produce up to 1 billion cubic feet per day (cf/d) of natural gas to increase ADNOC’s total output to 6 billion cf/d. The additional gas from the field will be used mainly as feedstock for new power, desalination and petrochemical plants in Abu Dhabi and the northern Emirates, with the remainder being reinjected into Abu Dhabi’s oil fields to maintain reservoir pressure and production. Find out more about positioning systems.
 
New Engineering Manager
After an absence of 7 years, Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that Brian Simister re-joined us on the 2nd January in the position of Engineering Manager. In addition to managing the contract engineering team, Brian will head up the R&D team to ensure that Paladon Systems maintains its technology lead with regards to its more advanced technology such as its hydraulic power units and electro-hydraulic positional systems. Brian Simister, “After leaving Paladon Systems in 2006 I went to work for another actuator manufacturer in the roles of Engineering Manager and Engineering Director. I was mainly involved with the development of gearboxes and pneumatic systems; however, both of these technologies are relatively straightforward and I started to miss the challenge of working with Paladon Systems' more advanced product solutions. Paladon Systems’ global presence has increased significantly since 2006 and so I am excited to not only be involved with maintaining Paladon Systems' technology lead, but also in being able to apply it on a rapidly expanding international basis.”
 

Paladon Systems Enhances Customer Support for Pipeline Projects
On 19th November Stuart Bailey re-joined Paladon Systems in the role of Sales Manager - Pipeline Equipment to provide sales and aftersales support for our clients involved in pipelines. Previously working at Paladon Systems in the role of Northern Area Sales Manager until 2006, Stuart has spent the last 6 years working for rma Pipeline Equipment to provide various product solutions for the pipeline industry. Managing Director Brian Ennever, “We predict significant investment in Europe’s pipeline infrastructure in the next few years. Stuart’s previous experience with pipeline valve automation solutions means that we will be able to work more closely than ever with our clients during a project’s design phase to make sure that they have access to the latest cost-effective and environmentally responsible valve automation technologies.” Stuart Bailey, “Paladon Systems has developed a lot of new and industry-leading valve automation technology in the 6 years I’ve been away. I’m looking forward to introducing the new technology to our clients and helping them integrate it into their pipeline upgrade projects.”
