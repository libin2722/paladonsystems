   July 2015, Issue 19

Dear Visitor,

We are now in the final quarter of our Financial Year and, while it will not be another record breaker as we’ve enjoyed for the last three years, the result we anticipate is quite reasonable given the current trading situation.
 
The past few months have been busy with trips to Kuala Lumpur, Tehran and two days at the Industrial Valve Summit in Bergamo, Italy. The highlight of the Malaysian Oil and Gas Show was the formal signing of an agreement with our local business partner Songket Petroleum. The signing was witnessed by several senior managers of Petronas who have recently granted us our local manufacturing licence. 
 
By way of relaxation we enjoyed the company of customers at the British Open Golf at St Andrews last weekend. Although the weather conspired against us and no actual golf was played, we managed to thoroughly enjoy a very sociable day.
 
I thank you all for your continued support which is enabling us to further invest in technology and resources. I look forward to being in contact with you again in our October newsletter.
 
Brian Ennever
First Major Contract for Compact Electro-Hydraulic Systems
After first unveiling the technology at Valve World last December, Paladon Systems has been awarded an order for 154 compact linear ring-main electro-hydraulic systems. These ultra-compact systems feature an integrated positioner and feedback transducer and will provide positional choke valve control as well as a fail-safe mode on loss of either the command signal or electrical power supply. Business & Product Development Manager Mike Northwood, “Following the successful recent delivery to Koso of the World’s first hydraulic positioning systems with Foundation Fieldbus communications, we are pleased that Koso has also trusted us with this prestigious contract.”

World’s First Foundation Fieldbus Electro-Hydraulic Systems Delivered
Mounted horizontally and fitted to 6” Class 1500 and 12” Class 900 choke valves, these 94 ring-main electro-hydraulic systems will be providing stepping valve control with an accuracy of ±0.5%. Senior Sales Engineer Kalam Mohammad, “This was our first major order with Koso and we are delighted that we have successfully delivered these systems and also gained yet another engineering first in valve automation.” Find out more about Koso and electro-hydraulic valve positioning systems.

11 Years of Global Field Service Experience Added to Engineering
After 11 years’ experience as a Workshop Technician and Site Services Technician at Paladon Systems, we are pleased to announce that Steve Piper recently has been promoted to the position of Design Engineer. Engineering Director Brian Simister, “Steve’s hands-on and global field experience gives him excellent insight into the operational challenges and needs of our customers. His experience will be invaluable in helping us further develop our product ranges to ensure that they continue to meet or exceed our customers’ expectations; particularly with respect to reliability and low maintenance requirements.”

Customer Appreciation Day
High winds greeted us at the 144th British Open at St Andrews. Hourly updates were issued from the Organisers but it was 6.00pm before there was any play. We did manage to see some high profile players including the eventual winner Zach Johnson on the 16th and 17th holes. For the golfing purists it was not a good day, however, for the Paladon Systems table, we had a great time!
