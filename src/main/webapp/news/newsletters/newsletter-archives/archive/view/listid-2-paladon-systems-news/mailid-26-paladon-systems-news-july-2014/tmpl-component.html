   July 2014, Issue 15

Dear Visitor,

Life continues apace. Since our last newsletter in April we exhibited at OTC in Houston with Paladon Americas where we launched our new solar powered self-contained electro-hydraulic fail-safe system. We also exhibited at the Tehran Oil and Gas Show in May and renewed old acquaintances in light of the improving political relationships between our two countries. On a lighter note, earlier this month we enjoyed a day of golf with our customers at the Scottish Open in Aberdeen.

We have also just become an ACE Partner of Auma (see below) and are subsequently now able to offer a complete range of advanced pneumatic, hydraulic and electric actuators and control systems to UK Oil & Gas companies.

Paladon Italia is now very close to procuring a new facility in Italy which will immediately allow us to double our manufacturing capacity and allow for additional expansion in the coming years; more details to follow shortly.

Brian Ennever
 
Turkmenistan Offshore Project Win
Paladon Systems to supply 13 self-contained electro-hydraulic systems for the Garagol Deniz West Offshore Facilities project. To be fitted to 6” Class 10,000 choke valves, these compact self-contained electro-hydraulic systems will provide valve positional control with an accuracy of ±0.5% or better. Remote control will be provided via a 4 - 20mA signal using HART communications and local control will be provided via push button stations and an infrared handheld controller. Find out more about Paladon Systems self-contained electro-hydraulic systems.
 
Paladon Systems Ships its Largest Ever Order for China
Comprising of 167 self-contained hydraulic valve automation systems fitted to 2” to 6” class 900 to 2500 ball valves, Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that it has shipped its largest order from a Chinese customer in its 33 year history. Senior Contract Engineer Mary Shepherd, “The sheer quantity of units coupled with the relatively complex control systems made this one of the most logistically challenging contracts we've had so far this year; however, the excellent support from our Chinese agent (Beijing Safewell Technology) helped ensure its successful completion.” Find out more about Paladon Systems’ hydraulic scotch-yoke valve actuators.
 
ACE Partnership Signed with Auma
After assisting Auma with achieving National Grid’s Electric Actuator VA2 approval, Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that they are now an official ACE (Auma Certified Engineering) partner of Auma. Paladon Systems will be responsible for Auma valve actuator sales and after sales support for UK Oil and Gas companies including National Grid, Scotia Gas Networks, Wales & West Utilities and Northern Gas Networks. Headquartered in Germany, Auma has been designing and manufacturing electric valve actuators for over 45 years and currently have over 2,300 employees in 30 locations worldwide. Find out more about Auma.
 
514th Valve Actuator & Control System for Karachaganak Field Shipped
Fitted to a 6” Class 2500 gate valve requiring high thrusts but with significant valve actuator size constraints, Paladon Systems designed a dual 74cm diameter stacked pneumatic piston arrangement for the spring-return actuator that is able to generate up to 210 kN and 225 kN on the pneumatic and spring strokes respectively. Paladon Systems has provided over 514 systems to the Karachaganak field since 1999 to operate 1” to 20” ball and gate valves. Find out more about Paladon Systems linear valve actuators.
 
Applications Engineering Support Increased for the Americas
Paladon Systems is pleased to announce the appointment of Roger Burnison in the position of Senior Engineering Consultant for the Americas. Working closely with Paladon Americas, Roger’s primary responsibility will be to provide engineering support to engineering houses, EPCs and end users across the Americas when bespoke and highly engineered valve automation solutions are required. Roger, “Paladon Systems’ self-contained electro/hydraulic technology is being rapidly adopted in Europe and the Far East; however, many users in the Americas continue to rely on older and less reliable valve automation technologies such as gas-over-oil and direct gas systems. I’m looking forward to meeting with engineering teams across the continent to show how this type of technology can help increase their process uptime as well as reducing their operating costs and the impact that their processes have on the environment.”
 
Paladon Italia Supports Increased Power Generation in Egypt
Paladon Italia recently shipped pneumatic spring-return linear valve actuators to be used in the 650 MW gas/oil fired power unit for the Suez Thermal Power Plant. The valve actuators will be fitted to 8” Class 2500 and 4” Class 900 Cesare Bonetti gate valves and will provide remote valve control as well as local manual operation via handwheel overrides. Find out more about Cesare Bonetti and Paladon Systems turbine bypass systems.
  
New Hire for Paladon Systems' Headquarters
Paladon Systems is pleased to announce the hire of Sam Webb in the role of Workshop Technician. Having previously worked for one of Paladon Systems’ technology partners for the last 10 years, Sam is already fully qualified on Paladon Systems’ industry-leading TVC Positioner and PST Controller technologies. Sam holds various BTEC and NVQ qualifications including ones in Mechanical Engineering, Electrical/Electronic Engineering and IT Practitioners. His hobbies include football and mountain biking.
 
Paladon Systems to Implement New ERP System
Order lead times to be reduced and customer service levels increased following the implementation of Microsoft Dynamics NAV on 1st August. ERP Implementation Coordinator Michael Aveling, “It’s been exciting to see the progression of the project and, now that the hardware upgrades have been installed and the personnel training completed, we’re in good shape to quickly reap the benefits of our investment when we go live on 1st August. Considering the expected numerous process efficiency gains across our Purchasing, Contract and Engineering departments as well as the two Workshops at our English headquarters, we will be able to significantly improve upon the already high levels of service currently enjoyed by our clients.” Find out more about Microsoft Dynamics NAV.
