Theddlethorpe Gas Terminal Project Win
To be fitted to 3” to 20” Class 300 and 600 ball valves, Paladon Systems will be supplying 29 pneumatic spring-return scotch-yoke actuators and control systems to provide ESD capability on loss of electrical or pneumatic supply sources. Each actuator will be fitted with dual solenoid valves and a closed loop breathing system to prevent environmental ingress during the actuator's spring stroke. In addition, actuators mounted to larger valves will be fitted with quick exhaust valves to ensure closing speeds of no more than 1 second per inch of valve size. Theddlethorpe Gas Terminal is located on the Lincolnshire coast near Theddlethorpe St Helen and was originally constructed in 1972 to receive gas from the Viking gas field. Today the terminal receives gas from 21 North Sea gas fields and provides approximately 10% of the UK’s total natural gas requirements. Find out more about Paladon Systems scotch-yoke actuators.
 
SIRA SIL 3 Approved Actuators
Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that their pneumatic and hydraulic quarter-turn and linear spring-return actuators have been approved by SIRA for use in SIL 2 and SIL 3 applications. Paladon Systems Engineering Manager Brian Simister, “Although our internal functional safety calculations clearly proved that our actuators were suitable for SIL 3 applications and despite the fact that we have previously supplied them for use in numerous SIL 3 certified systems, the lack of formal 3rd party approval was causing confusion with some of our potential customers. We feel proud that SIRA has now validated our earlier functional safety calculations and given their formal approval to use our actuators in SIL 3 certified applications.” Download the SIRA certificate or the complete functional safety document.
 
North Azadegan Oilfield Project Win
To be fitted to 2” to 6” Class 900 to 2500 ball valves, Paladon Systems will be supplying 167 self-contained hydraulic valve automation systems to provide valve fail close capability on loss of an electric command signal or in the event of a fire. Vice President Sales Asia Pacific Region Anba Nagappan, “This is a significant project win for us and our Chinese agent Beijing Safewell Technology due to the project’s size and the highly competitive bidding phase. Beijing Safewell Technology’s close working relationship with China National Petroleum Corporation (CNPC) and their outstanding performance throughout the competitive technical and commercial processes were instrumental in helping us secure this prestigious order.” Beijing Safewell Technology’s Commercial Manager Shirley Yan, “We are delighted to have won this project with Paladon Systems. Their vast experience with bespoke hydraulic systems enabled them to quickly develop numerous designs throughout the bidding process to adapt to the project’s changing technical requirements and to ensure that the accepted design met fully with the project’s final technical requirements. The development of the surface facilities at the North Azadegan oilfield will continue in multiple phases in the coming years and we look forward to the opportunity of supporting CNPC during these future enhancements.” Find out more about Paladon Systems self-contained electro-hydraulic systems.
