Dear Visitor,
 
 
It is my pleasure to announce that this quarter we reached our 10th anniversary of being an actuator manufacturer. For the first 20 years we purchased actuators from other manufacturers and concentrated solely on designing control systems; however, a decade ago we embarked on designing and manufacturing our own comprehensive range of pneumatic and hydraulic quarter-turn and linear actuators.
 
 
Today, we have well over 10,000 actuators installed around the globe and without a single failure. What makes Paladon Systems actuators one of the most technically advanced in the world is the fact that our actuators were designed this century from the ground-up using the latest design tools, materials, manufacturing and testing techniques; whereas many of our competitor designs were originally conceived over 50 years ago. Actuator R&D progresses at our Italian manufacturing plant and our UK headquarters continues to build on over 30 years of control system design experience, allowing us to offer our customers reliable and cost effective systems that incorporate the latest digital communication protocols and PLC technology. This actuator and controls experience also allows us to fulfil the most critical applications in the world today, including fully certified turnkey onshore, offshore and subsea HIPPS systems.
 
 
As we enter our final financial quarter, we are on course to have a record year in terms of turnover and global experience with exports now making up over 80% of our business.
 
 
Thank you all for your support and I’ll be in contact with you again in October.
 
Brian Ennever

Paladon Systems is pleased to announce the renewal of a 3 year frame agreement with Occidental Qatar for the supply of offshore actuators and control systems. Fitted to 3” to 6” Class 600 choke valves manufactured by In Line Valve Company, Paladon Systems will continue to be the sole supplier for all of Occidental’s electro-hydraulic valve positioning systems. Product Manager Mike Northwood, “After supplying nearly 100 systems during the last three years without a single failure, we are delighted that Occidental Qatar continues to demonstrate their confidence in our valve positioning technology.” Find out more about Paladon Systems hydraulic power units and valve positioning systems.
 
Paladon Systems to Supply BP with Stainless Steel Actuators
Paladon Systems’ experience with critical applications in the most extreme environmental conditions has resulted in them being awarded an order for the supply of 65 stainless steel spring-return scotch-yoke actuators. Fitted to 3” Class 600 ball valves, the actuators will be supplied with control systems to allow for remote on/off operation and to ensure that the valves close on either loss of the pneumatic supply pressure or electrical control signal. Sales Manager, Bob Curnow, “These units will be installed in a sand separation system on the West Azeri platform that is located off the coast of Baku in Azerbaijan. The operating environment is extremely corrosive and the application so critical that BP specified that all pressure containing parts of the actuators were to be manufactured from 316 stainless steel and sized to provide twice the actual valve torque requirements. BP's decision to move to stainless steel actuators was made after numerous failures of carbon steel actuators previously supplied by other manufacturers. We look forward to commencing shipments in September." Find out more about Paladon Systems scotch-yoke actuators.
 
Dual Spring Linear Actuators Supplied for PNG LNG Project
For several months Paladon Systems has been busy completing the supply of 25 electro-hydraulic valve positioning systems for the Esso Highlands project in Papua New Guinea. Powered by a ring-main biodegradable fluid supply, the actuators and controls provide precise valve positioning via HART communication protocol for 2” to 8” valves up to 2500 pressure class. Product Manager Mike Northwood, “One of the biggest challenges on this project was providing linear hydraulic actuators that met the unusual thrust characteristics of the control valves. Twenty three of the valves required a very high break to close thrust but also an extremely low end to close (reseat) thrust. The specifications originally called for double-acting actuators; however, it is was quickly determined that any double-acting actuators that provided sufficient break to close thrust would also provide too much reseat thrust, possibly resulting in damage to the valves. To overcome this we designed spring-return actuators that open the valves on the hydraulic stroke and close them on the spring stroke using springs with very low reseat thrusts. The specifications required that the valves would fail stay-put on loss of the actuator’s hydraulic supply pressure, so we included automatic isolation valves that lock the hydraulic supply pressure in the actuators once pressure from the ring-main circuit was lost and additional circuitry to allow for thermal expansion in the closed system. These units have now been shipped and most already installed. The remaining 2 valves required spring-return fail-open actuators that also had very unusual valve opening thrusts that necessitated a bespoke nested spring arrangement to provide a non-linear actuator thrust output on the valves opening strokes.” Find out more about linear actuators and electro-hydraulic systems.
   
Landmark Power Generation Project Win
Recently Paladon Systems received an order from GE Energy in France for its first turbine bypass system for a Malaysian project, and also the first for a project engineered by EPC Power Alstom. Paladon Systems will be supplying 3 Hydraulic Power Units and 3 linear valve actuators with positioning systems to protect the steam turbines in the 1,000MW ultra-supercritical coal-fired power plant. The Manjung 4 power plant will be the largest single unit in South East Asia and capable of providing power to nearly 2 million homes. Product Manager Mike Northwood, “This is an exciting first for us and one that demonstrates that our advanced turbine bypass technology is being seen globally as the way forward in the conventional power generation industry. One of the features of our advanced technology is that our turbine bypass systems use much less energy than the traditional technologies and this feature compliments Power Alstom’s ultra-supercritical environmentally responsible technology that also provides operators with lower OPEX. This technology also allows operators to run these types of power plants at higher temperatures than conventional coal-fired power plants, resulting in improved efficiency and lower NOx and SOx emissions.” Find out more about turbine bypass systems.
