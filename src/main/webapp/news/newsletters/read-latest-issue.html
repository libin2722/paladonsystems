Paladon Systems News - October 2016

Having trouble reading this newsletter? Click here to view it online

    October 2016, Issue 24

Dear Visitor,

Our year end passed at the end of September and I’m very glad to see the back of what has not been a great year; but hey ho, that’s life. Fortunately our new year has started quite well as you will read below.
 
Our win of the BP West Nile Delta project is significant in that the safety engineering aspect of the project has taken a big step forward in terms of recognising the potential areas of failure associated, historically, with the final control elements. Paladon Systems’ ability to engineer a system to meet the ever increasing safety demands made a significant contribution to our success on this project.
 
We continue to push forward with our development programs and are pleased to announce that, following successful trials in the North Midlands, UK, a stand-alone solar-powered electro-hydraulic actuator will be installed in a live gas pipeline application. The performance will be monitored closely throughout the winter period, but we are very confident in our technology and about the final outcome. 
 
We are now entering a busy period of exhibitions, with ADIPEC in Abu Dhabi and Valve World in Düsseldorf taking us up to the Christmas holidays.
 
May I thank you again for your continued support.
 
Brian Ennever
Meet Us Next Month at ADIPEC & Valve World
Paladon Systems will be exhibiting from 7th to 10th November at ADIPEC (Hall 6, Stand 6150) and from 29th November to 1st December at Valve World (Hall 5, Stand 5L34). Make an appointment to meet with us and see the latest developments in valve automation technology in action.
Largest Project Win to Date in 2016
BP and Bechtel select Paladon Systems for the West Nile Delta project. Comprising of orders from 4 valve companies and 1 packaged process plant manufacturer, Paladon Systems has been selected to supply pneumatic and self-contained electro-hydraulic rotary and linear actuator systems. Currently the total scope of award is for over 100 systems; however, the final scope is expected to rise to approximately 400. Sales Manager, Bob Curnow, “Considering that West Nile Delta is the only significant EPC project currently being engineered in the UK, we are particularly proud to have won this order. In addition to providing an outstanding level of customer service throughout the entire bidding process, it didn’t hurt that we also have an excellent 28 year track-record with BP projects.” Find out more about the West Nile Delta project.
Control Valve Solutions Partnership
Paladon Systems’ cutting-edge valve automation technologies and Control Valve Solutions (CVS) best-in-class field service support combine to provide unparalleled support for their clients in Scotland and the North Sea. CVS maintains a team of highly trained and talented engineers and technicians who are available to go offshore, both within the UK and internationally, or work on valves within CVS' extensive workshops in Aberdeen. CVS can overhaul, repair and supply all types of valves, including control, ball, butterfly, safety relief, and choke valves. Find out more about CVS.
Al-Arfaj Engineering Partners with Paladon Systems in Kuwait
Al-Arfaj Engineering Co. WLL (AEC) was established in 1987 to provide piping material, process instrumentation and rotating equipment to the Kuwaiti oil and petrochemical industries, as well as equipment and spares to the local power and water industries. After nearly 3 decades of continuous growth and innovation, the Company now has a diversified portfolio of products and services in sectors that include petrochemical engineering, oil & gas, environment & pollution monitoring. Find out more about AEC.
Enterprising UK Magazine Article
Find out how Paladon Systems has grown from its foundation in 1981 into a global concern focused on customer service and the development of pioneering valve automation technologies. Read the article and find out more about Enterprising UK.
Paladon Systems to Help Meet Europe's Growing Energy Needs
Paladon Systems has been awarded an order for the Trans Anatolian Natural Gas Pipeline Project (TANAP).To be fitted to 1” to 6” control valves, Paladon Systems will supply 5 double-acting and 11 spring-return linear self-contained electro-hydraulic valve positioning systems. The compact manifolded control systems will provide precise 4-20mA valve positioning control and include accumulators to allow for 2 valve strokes via manual operation of solenoid valves should the electrical power supply be lost. Find out more about TANAP and valve positioning systems.
New Indian Agent
Paladon Systems is pleased to announce that Indian Devices & Engineering Corporation (IDEC) has joined Paladon Systems global network of agents and valve automation centres. IDEC is a leading manufacturers' representative and value-added distributor for several high-technology industries, particularly India’s Oil & Gas Industry. From its centrally-located headquarters in Mumbai's business district, IDEC handles over a hundred enquiries every month for flow-control equipment and other specialized technologies sought by the Oil & Gas industry. Contact IDEC.
